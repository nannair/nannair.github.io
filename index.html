My Idea

Overview

My idea involves the creation of an 8-bit mobile game which relies on the player getting out of the house, much like Pokemon go. Much like modern games, there are different ranks that a player can achieve by leveling up, but they would need to battle with other players in order to do so. It would be a fantasy game with a rather simple artistic style, especially in the battle screen, which is inspired by Mortal Kombat and the game boy Pokemon games. This game would be aimed at youth from ages ten to eighteen, as well as adults, since there would be little to no fantasy violence within the game.

Motivation

The motivation behind this game was because of the increase prevalence of obesity within countries such as Australia and the United States. Statistics show that if the population reduced their body mass index by 1*, fourteen percent of the burden of disease in the future could be avoided. Walking around in order to search for battles would give a substantial workout to those who play, instead of them staying at home in order to wait for online matches. This would also motivate the players to create more real-life connections, rather than making (possibly unsafe) virtual connections from other countries. This could be especially good for modern youth, considering they tend to spend more time at home, on their laptops.

Description

There are many services that collaborate to make this game, and it seems to be a rather complex project. Much like any combat-type game, there will be multiple champions for a player to choose from and to purchase with in-game money -- all of these would be inspired by fantasy creatures, such as elves, mages, vampires, et cetera. The players would have to physically get out of their house and communicate with others in order to level themselves up, otherwise they are able to battle with bots, which would earn them in-game currency, but will not give them experience points in order to level up.

These battles would have the same format as a Pokemon battle, with four choices -- two attack, and two defenses. One move would be a power move that lands critical hits but can only be used when a certain bar is at its maximum. During each turn, both players would choose an option, and when the time is up, their moves will be displayed, and according to their moves, their health will either decrease or stay the same. There will be three rounds, and the winner will gain more experience points than the loser. These experience points can be utilized to purchase more powerful champions.

Initiating battle is where NFC comes in -- by tapping one’s phone against another player’s phone, they immediately go into a champion select screen, in which they can choose a character to fight as. They are able to exit, but only with the permission of the second player. A five-second timer would be displayed on screen when a player has not responded in a while; the reason it is so short is because these competitions are active, and much like the old board game, Battleships, each player is sitting substantially close to each other while engaging in battle. Switching off the Bluetooth during this battle would also immediately exit a player out of the game, and thus, give them an ‘away from keyboard’ or ‘AFK’ warning; after five warnings, a player will have a certain waiting time if wanting to engage in bot battles.

There will be a GPS which utilizes a player’s current location in order to find new opponents; theirs would be tagged with their avatar and arrow over their location. Within the in-game map, recognized landmarks will act as ‘stations’ from which people can stand a chance to win items such as armor, health potions, or mana potions.

In-game items, which can be won from landmarks or purchased from the store, are equipment that can be used during a battle or equipped before a battle. These items often mess with the stats of a character. For example, armor can be equipped before a battle (chosen to equip within the champion select screen) and will increase the defense of the player; unfortunately, it can only be used for one entire battle. Other items such as health and mana potions would be utilized during a battle and would be a ‘use item’ choice. If they choose this, they cannot attack or defend.

Other notable features include in-game chat to message friends, and information boxes for each character.

Tools and Technologies

One of the main technologies which are needed in order to make this is Near Field Communication, which is a wireless protocol, similar to Bluetooth, it can be used to exchange information between two devices. Unlike its traditional use of data transfer, this chip would be used to immediately connect two devices to each other via Bluetooth. However, this game relies on both phones to have an NFC chip, otherwise, the phones can be connected manually via phone settings. GPS would also be another key technology, since it is needed to track the locations of players -- location would have to be switched on in order for tracking to work.

Skills Required

Knowledge of programming is required to make this game, which included fluency in Java and Python -- these would be the main bases of creating the entire video game, including every command, action, and option. A team member also needs to be able to manage the database of the game, since players would have to make an account in order to play and track their progress. Lastly, graphic designers are needed in order to create characters, environments, and the overall colorful visuals of the video game. Since the game is 2-D, they would need to know how to use Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator in order to create logos and characters.

Outcome

The main outcome I am aiming for with this video game is to motivate people to engage with their community and get out of their rooms more. Those who play this game would easily connect with their neighbors and would also allow families to spend time together. Unlike other violent video games, this game would not negatively impact the psyche of growing teenagers, since the art style is far from realistic and does not involve them yelling curse words to strangers they have never met. Adults who are desperate to move up to better ranks (which easily comes with games like these) would take effort to manually travel to the nearest ‘stations’ or player.
