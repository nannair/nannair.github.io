Interest in IT

What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start? Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).

My interest and enthusiasm in IT ranges from interactive media and game development, since I very much enjoy watching gameplays, watching the process by which games are created, and playing them as well. My interest in IT began when I watched the gameplay for ‘Beyond: Two Souls’, a video game developed by Quantic dream. It was picturesque and had a very interesting premise; but what more mesmerized me was the fact that they used real actors and green-screened props in order to create and entire environment and different characters. I was only 13 years old at that time, and I had been inspired to learn CGI and/or the programming that went with creating 3D video games. My experience first started when I was around 5 years old, when I used to play educational games, which were all developed, in the early 90s, from one single brand called Knowledge adventure. It then continued on to flash games, which are still found on websites such as nitrome, miniclip, etc. A gaming platform called Steam had then raised in popularity around 2014/2015, and that was when I started to game on the PC. The rest of my experiences also range from Microsoft applications when doing school project work, and also adobe photoshop for digital drawing.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

RMIT is in the top 1% of the best universities in the world, and is specialized in technology, which is the field I am highly interested in. Studying in the city also sounds very exciting, even if it takes a long time to travel to the campus. The professors were also said to be very helpful and passionate about the subject they are teaching, as well as the tutors who assist students during their tute labs and practicals. They also seem to have a games and interactive media course linked to the Information Technology program, which I am keen on joining in my second year, if it is possible. RMIT is the best for graphics, design, and technology, and those are three areas I want to excel in, since I am very interested in them.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

From my studies, I expect to learn how to program at least a 2-D platformer video game, and create large interactive virtual environments. I want to be able to make a video game that is story-based, with interactive non-player characters and items, as well as learn how to work as part of a team of video game developers in order to create one huge project. A huge accomplishment would be to learn teamwork and cooperation when working with people you are not familiar with. I aim to improve my communication skills, and also extend my creativity past its boundaries.
